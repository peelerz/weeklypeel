Painting of the week

Tuco Amalfi is a brazilian painter of fantasy surrealist from Sao Paulo. He arouses in us more attention and perception, take us to the learning and demand for the magnitude of the simple things of everyday life and record his testimony of admiration for the Universe mystery. Tuco is a multimedia artist, that utilizes sculptures, furniture, objects; but it is in the painting and in the drawing his strongest manifest of art expression, brings us to a reflection about Love, Wisdom and Faith.

Drawing of the week

Laurie Lipton was born in New York and began drawing at the age of four. She was inspired by the religious paintings of the Flemish School. Unfortunately she went to university when Conceptual Art was all the rage & no one could teach her how to paint like an early Renaissance Master. She tried to teach herself from books and failed. While traveling around Europe in her 20's, she began developing her very own peculiar drawing technique building up tone with thousands of fine cross-hatching lines like an egg tempera painting. Laurie is determined to address any subject which holds personal meaning for her, no matter how challenging or uncomfortable it may be for viewers. She is constantly pushing limits of herself and her audience.

Collage of the week

Albane Simon is a Parisian illustrator, digital collagist who teleports her imagination into a world populated by strange surrealist figures. At the crossroads between vintage and fantasy, she tackles apocalyptic subjects, theatricalizing them through a rocambolic staging, between ancient tragedy and futuristic delirium, which teems with evocative details. Proving her talent on the musical territory working with Gaslamp Killer, Albane exploits themes as varied as ecology, architecture and spirituality. Hes work has been exhibited on numerous occasions in London, Paris and Athens.
